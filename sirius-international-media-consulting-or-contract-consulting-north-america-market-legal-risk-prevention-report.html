## Executive Summary

 

The North American market (United States and Canada), as one of the world's largest economies, features a comprehensive but complex legal system and business environment. For Chinese enterprises seeking to enter or already operating in the region, contract management is a core component of legal risk prevention and commercial interest protection. Based on Sirius International Media Consulting's global legal consulting experience, this report provides an in-depth analysis of the North American market contract legal environment, common risk points, and prevention strategies, offering professional contract management recommendations to help enterprises achieve steady development in the North American market.

## 1. North America Market Contract Legal Environment Overview

 

### 1.1 US Contract Law System

 

**Legal System Characteristics:**

- Common law system: Primarily case law, supplemented by statutory law

- Federal system: Coexistence of federal and state laws, significant state differences

- Precedent binding force: Precedents have binding force on subsequent cases

 

**Major Legal Sources:**

  1. **Federal Law:**

   - Uniform Commercial Code (UCC): Regulates commercial transactions

   - Federal regulations: Specific industry regulations

   - Federal precedents: Federal court precedents

 

  1. **State Law:**

   - State contract laws: Basic contract rules

   - State commercial laws: Commercial transaction rules

   - State precedents: State court precedents

 

  1. **International Treaties:**

   - United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods (CISG): US is a party

   - Bilateral investment treaties: China-US investment protection treaties

 

### 1.2 Canadian Contract Law System

 

**Legal System Characteristics:**

- Mixed legal system: Common law system (except Quebec) and civil law system (Quebec)

- Federal system: Coexistence of federal and provincial laws

- Bilingual environment: Both English and French are official languages

 

**Major Legal Sources:**

  1. **Federal Law:**

   - Federal commercial law: Interprovincial commercial transactions

   - Federal regulations: Specific industry regulations

 

  1. **Provincial Law:**

   - Provincial contract laws: Basic contract rules

   - Quebec Civil Code: Civil law contract rules

 

  1. **International Treaties:**

   - United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods (CISG): Canada is a party

 

### 1.3 Contract Law Basic Principles

 

**Core Principles:**

  1. **Freedom of Contract:** Parties can freely negotiate contract terms
  2. **Consideration Principle:** Contracts must have valid consideration
  3. **Good Faith:** Parties should perform contracts in good faith
  4. **Enforceability:** Contracts must have legal binding force

 

**Contract Formation Elements:**

- Offer

- Acceptance

- Consideration

- Intent to create legal relations

- Capacity

## 2. Common Contract Types and Risk Points

 

### 2.1 Sales and Purchase Contracts

 

**Major Terms:**

- Product description and specifications

- Price and payment terms

- Delivery and acceptance

- Quality assurance and warranty

- Breach of contract liability

- Dispute resolution

 

**Risk Point Analysis:**

  1. **Product Liability Risk:**

   - Product defects causing personal injury or property damage

   - Strict liability principle: Liability may exist even without fault

   - Punitive damages: May face high punitive damages

 

  1. **Delivery Risk:**

   - Unclear delivery terms: FOB, CIF and other trade term selection

   - Delivery time delays: Breach liability and damages

   - Delivery location disputes: Title transfer timing

 

  1. **Quality Assurance Risk:**

   - Unclear quality standards: Acceptance standard disputes

   - Warranty period and scope: Warranty responsibility definition

   - Implied warranties: Statutory implied warranties

 

  1. **Payment Risk:**

   - Unfavorable payment terms: Excessive advance payment ratio

   - Exchange rate risk: Foreign currency settlement exchange rate fluctuations

   - Credit risk: Buyer default risk

 

### 2.2 Distribution and Agency Contracts

 

**Major Terms:**

- Authorization scope and territory

- Exclusivity terms

- Sales targets and quotas

- Pricing and discounts

- Advertising and promotion

- Termination terms

 

**Risk Point Analysis:**

  1. **Exclusivity Risk:**

   - Exclusive agency rights granted: Restricting own development

   - Territory protection: Unclear territory division

   - Exclusivity obligations: Constraints on distributors

 

  1. **Antitrust Risk:**

   - Resale price maintenance (RPM): Fixing resale prices may be illegal

   - Territorial restrictions: Excessive territorial restrictions may be illegal

   - Tying requirements: Mandatory tying may be illegal

 

  1. **Termination Risk:**

   - Unclear termination conditions: Termination right disputes

   - Termination notice period: Termination notice period too short or too long

   - Post-termination obligations: Inventory handling, customer transfer

 

  1. **Intellectual Property Risk:**

   - Trademark use: Trademark authorization scope

   - Trade secret protection: Confidentiality obligations

   - IP infringement: Distributor infringement liability

 

### 2.3 Licensing and Franchise Contracts

 

**Major Terms:**

- License scope and duration

- License fees and payment

- Quality control

- Training and support

- Intellectual property protection

- Termination terms

 

**Risk Point Analysis:**

  1. **Intellectual Property Risk:**

   - IP validity: Whether licensor's IP is valid

   - Infringement risk: Licensee may infringe third-party rights

   - Improvement ownership: Ownership of improvement results

 

  1. **Quality Control Risk:**

   - Quality standards: Quality control requirements

   - Audit rights: Licensor audit rights

   - Brand protection: Brand image maintenance

 

  1. **Fee Risk:**

   - Fee structure: Fixed fees vs. royalty fees

   - Fee adjustment: Fee adjustment mechanisms

   - Dispute resolution: Fee dispute resolution

 

### 2.4 Service Contracts

 

**Major Terms:**

- Service scope and standards

- Service duration and renewal

- Service fees and payment

- Service Level Agreement (SLA)

- Intellectual property ownership

- Confidentiality obligations

 

**Risk Point Analysis:**

  1. **Service Scope Risk:**

   - Unclear scope: Service content disputes

   - Change management: Service change procedures

   - Scope creep: Additional service charges

 

  1. **Service Level Risk:**

   - SLA metrics: Service level metric setting

   - Breach liability: Consequences of service level non-compliance

   - Damage limitations: Damage limit setting

 

  1. **Intellectual Property Risk:**

   - Background IP: Parties' existing IP

   - Foreground IP: Ownership of newly generated IP

   - License use: IP license scope

 

### 2.5 Joint Venture and Partnership Contracts

 

**Major Terms:**

- Capital contribution and equity structure

- Corporate governance

- Management and operations

- Profit distribution

- Exit mechanisms

- Dispute resolution

 

**Risk Point Analysis:**

  1. **Governance Structure Risk:**

   - Equity ratio: Unreasonable equity distribution

   - Board composition: Board seat allocation

   - Decision-making mechanisms: Major matter decision procedures

 

  1. **Capital Contribution Risk:**

   - Contribution methods: Cash, assets, IP contributions

   - Contribution valuation: Non-cash contribution valuation

   - Contribution breach: Insufficient contribution or breach

 

  1. **Exit Risk:**

   - Exit mechanisms: Exit conditions and procedures

   - Valuation methods: Valuation methods at exit

   - Right of first refusal: Equity transfer restrictions

- Set confidentiality period

- Specify breach consequences

- Consider technical protection measures

**Limitation Types:**

  1. **Exclusion Terms:**

   - Indirect damages: Indirect damage exclusion

  1. **Exclusions from Exclusion:**

   - Intentional acts: Intentional acts not excluded

**Best Practices:**

- Reasonably set liability caps

- Clearly define exclusion scope

- Consider insurance coverage

- Balance party risks
