# Sirius International Media Consulting | Market Research Eastern Europe Region Investment Opportunity Analysis

 

## Executive Summary

 

As an important bridge connecting Europe and Asia, the Eastern Europe region possesses a unique geographical location, rich natural resources, and continuously improving investment environment. With the deepening of the "Belt and Road" initiative, the Eastern Europe region has become an important destination for Chinese enterprises' overseas investment. Based on Sirius International Media Consulting's global market research capabilities, this report provides an in-depth analysis of the Eastern Europe region's business environment, investment opportunities, and risk challenges, providing data support and strategic guidance for enterprises to formulate investment strategies.

 

---

 

## 1. Eastern Europe Region Macro Environment Analysis

 

### 1.1 Geography and Population Overview

 

The Eastern-Europe region mainly includes Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary, Slovakia, Romania, Bulgaria and other countries, with a total area of approximately 1.3 million square kilometers and a total population of about 120 million.

 

**Major Country Overview:**

- Poland: Population ~38 million, GDP ~$680 billion, per capita GDP ~$18,000

- Czech Republic: Population ~10.7 million, GDP ~$290 billion, per capita GDP ~$27,000

- Hungary: Population ~9.7 million, GDP ~$180 billion, per capita GDP ~$19,000

- Slovakia: Population ~5.4 million, GDP ~$120 billion, per capita GDP ~$22,000

- Romania: Population ~19 million, GDP ~$280 billion, per capita GDP ~$15,000

- Bulgaria: Population ~6.9 million, GDP ~$80 billion, per capita GDP ~$12,000

 

### 1.2 Economic Development Status

 

**Economic Growth:**

- The Eastern Europe region maintains stable economic growth, with an annual growth rate of about 3-5%

- Poland has the largest economic scale and highest development level

- Czech Republic and Slovakia have relatively high economic development levels

- Romania and Bulgaria have high growth potential

 

**EU Membership:**

- All major Eastern European countries are EU members

- Enjoy advantages of the EU single market

- Receive EU structural fund support

- Use the euro (some countries)

 

### 1.3 Resource Endowment

 

**Natural Resources:**

- Agriculture: Fertile black soil, developed agriculture

- Minerals: Coal, uranium, non-ferrous metals

- Energy: Oil, natural gas, hydropower

- Forests: Rich forest resources

 

**Human Resources:**

- High education level, good labor quality

- Relatively lower labor costs compared to Western Europe

- Multilingual capabilities, high English proficiency

## 2. Eastern Europe Region Business Environment Analysis

 

### 2.1 Political Environment

 

**Political Systems:**

- All are democratic countries, relatively stable politics

- EU members, bound by EU law

- Friendly relations with China, continuously deepening cooperation

 

**Relations with China:**

- Important partner of "Belt and Road" initiative

- Member of "17+1 Cooperation" mechanism

- Continuous deepening of bilateral trade and investment cooperation

- China-Central and Eastern European Countries Cooperation Mechanism

 

### 2.2 Legal and Policy Environment

 

**Foreign Investment Policies:**

- Unified EU foreign investment policy

- Each country has enacted foreign investment laws, encouraging foreign investment

- Established special economic zones and industrial parks, offering preferential policies

- Protect legitimate rights and interests of investors

 

**Tax Policies:**

- Corporate income tax rates: Poland 19%, Czech Republic 19%, Hungary 9%, Slovakia 19%, Romania 16%, Bulgaria 10%

- VAT: EU unified VAT system, standard rate 20-25%

- Tax preferential policies: Special economic zones and specific industries enjoy tax preferences

 

**Trade Policies:**

- EU single market, free circulation of goods

- EU customs union, unified external tariffs

- Signed free trade agreements with multiple countries

 

### 2.3 Infrastructure

 

**Transportation Infrastructure:**

- Railway: Total length ~70,000 km, well-developed railway network

- Highway: Total length ~300,000 km, well-developed expressway network

- Aviation: Major cities have international airports, aviation network covers Europe

- Ports: Baltic Sea ports, connecting Northern and Western Europe

 

**Energy Infrastructure:**

- Electricity: Total installed capacity ~50 million kW

- Oil and natural gas: Well-developed pipeline network, connecting Russia and Western Europe

- Renewable energy: Rapid development of wind power and solar power

 

**Digital Infrastructure:**

- Internet penetration: Poland 85%, Czech Republic 90%, Hungary 85%, Slovakia 90%, Romania 75%, Bulgaria 70%

- Mobile communication: 4G network full coverage, 5G network deployment beginning

- Digital economy: All countries have formulated digital economy development plans

- Czech Republic: Škoda Auto headquarters, developed automotive manufacturing

- Slovakia: Kia, Hyundai have factories

 

**Electronics Manufacturing:**
