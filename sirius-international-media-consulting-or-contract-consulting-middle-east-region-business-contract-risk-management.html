## 1. Middle East Region Contract Legal Environment Overview

 

### 1.1 Legal System Characteristics

 

**Islamic Law Influence:**

- Islamic Sharia law is the foundation of legal systems in many countries

- Influences contract law, commercial law, family law, and other fields

- Has special requirements and restrictions on commercial activities

 

**Legal System Diversity:**

- Civil law system: Egypt, Turkey, and other countries

- Mixed legal system: Saudi Arabia, UAE, and other countries (Islamic law + civil law system)

- Common law system: Some countries influenced by common law

 

**International Treaty Participation:**

- United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods (CISG): Some countries are parties

- Bilateral investment treaties: Signed multiple investment protection treaties with China

- Regional trade agreements: Gulf Cooperation Council Free Trade Agreement, etc.

 

### 1.2 Major Country Legal Systems

 

**Saudi Arabia:**

- Legal system: Islamic law + royal decrees

- Contract law: Based on Islamic law principles

- Commercial law: Saudi Commercial Law

- Arbitration law: Saudi Arbitration Law

 

**United Arab Emirates:**

- Legal system: Civil law system + Islamic law

- Contract law: Based on civil law system

- Commercial law: UAE Commercial Law

- Arbitration law: UAE Federal Arbitration Law

 

**Qatar:**

- Legal system: Civil law system + Islamic law

- Contract law: Based on civil law system

- Commercial law: Qatar Commercial Law

- Arbitration law: Qatar Arbitration Law

 

**Egypt:**

- Legal system: Civil law system

- Contract law: Egyptian Civil Code

- Commercial law: Egyptian Commercial Code

- Arbitration law: Egyptian Arbitration Law

 

**Turkey:**

- Legal system: Civil law system

- Contract law: Turkish Civil Code

- Commercial law: Turkish Commercial Code

- Arbitration law: Turkish International Arbitration Law

 

### 1.3 Contract Law Basic Principles

 

**Islamic Law Principles:**

  1. **Prohibition of Interest (Riba):** Prohibition of charging and paying interest
  2. **Prohibition of Uncertainty (Gharar):** Prohibition of contracts with excessive uncertainty
  3. **Prohibition of Gambling (Maysir):** Prohibition of gambling-type contracts
  4. **Fair Trading:** Emphasis on fairness and justice

 

**Modern Contract Law Principles:**

  1. **Freedom of Contract:** Parties can freely negotiate contract terms
  2. **Good Faith:** Parties should perform contracts in good faith
  3. **Consideration:** Contracts should have consideration
  4. **Enforceability:** Contracts must have legal binding force
